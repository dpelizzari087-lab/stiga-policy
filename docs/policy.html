Privacy Policy

In accordance with article 13 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 — General Data Protection Regulation. This policy relates exclusively to the processing of personal data collected through the website www.stiga.com/int/.

1 Social Media

The Site uses social media widgets (e.g. Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, Pinterest, LinkedIn). Pursuant to data protection legislation, you are hereby informed that the widgets only transmit data relating to your browsing on the Site to the respective social media when you activate them by clicking on the corresponding button: the corresponding provider receives information that you visited our website with your IP address.

If you are simultaneously logged into your respective social media account, the corresponding provider can attribute visits to our pages to your user account to offer you personalised advertising content. The Company and the social media outlet concerned act as joint data controllers for these operations as defined in art. 26 of the GDPR. The legal basis for the processing carried out by Stiga is your consent, which can always be withdrawn by accessing the Cookie Policy.

3 Personal Data We Process

Stiga may process various types of personal data via the Site, depending on the specific purposes pursued. Examples include:

  • Personal Identifiable Information: first name, surname, address
  • Contact details: residential address, e-mail address, telephone number
  • Data on interests and preferences: data relating to your purchases (e.g. product code, value and type of purchase), products in your shopping cart
  • Transactional data: credit card number, tax code

Browsing data: The computer systems used to operate the Site collect certain personal data inherent to Internet communication protocols (e.g. IP addresses, domain names, URI addresses, time of request, server response codes, and other parameters relating to the user's operating system). These data are used solely to obtain anonymous statistical information and to check the Site's correct functioning; they are deleted immediately after processing. Data on web contacts are stored for no longer than seven days.

Cookies: Some pages of the Site use cookies, sent by Stiga or by third parties, to enable technical functioning, improve usability, and carry out statistical analysis. For further information, please refer to the Cookie Policy.

4 Purposes and Legal Bases of Processing

4.1 Handling your requests
Stiga processes data provided via the "Contact Us" form to reply to your specific request. No consent is required — processing is based on art. 6(1)(b) or (f) of the GDPR.

4.2 Marketing and soft spam
With your consent, Stiga may process your personal data to provide promotional information through newsletters, mailings, e-mails, telephone calls, text messages, posts on social networks, etc. You may withdraw consent at any time by writing to unsubscribe@stiga.com or clicking the unsubscribe link in any promotional e-mail. Legal basis: art. 6(1)(a) GDPR.

4.3 Preference analysis
If you have given consent, Stiga may analyse your data to learn about your purchasing preferences, improve commercial offerings and personalise communications. Legal basis: art. 6(1)(a) GDPR — consent may be withdrawn at any time.

4.4 Statistical analysis
Stiga processes data for statistical analysis purposes to improve its products and services. No consent is required — legal basis is the Controller's legitimate interest pursuant to art. 6(1)(f) GDPR.

4.5 "My Stiga" account and product registration
Stiga processes your personal data to allow you to create a personal account and access related services, including product registration and warranty activation. No consent is required — legal basis is art. 6(1)(b) GDPR.

4.6 E-commerce
When you make a purchase on the Website, your data is processed to complete the sales process, fulfil legal obligations, manage related services, and safeguard contractual rights. Legal bases: art. 6(1)(b), (c) and (f) GDPR.

5 Personal Data Retention Period

Your personal data will be kept for the time strictly necessary to pursue the purposes indicated in this Privacy Policy. Data will be destroyed or rendered anonymous upon expiry of the retention period.

Purpose Retention Period
4.1 — Handling requests As long as necessary to handle your request; max 5 years after your request.

6 Processing Procedures

Your personal data shall be processed in accordance with the GDPR, by means of paper, computer and telematic tools, in such a way as to guarantee their security and confidentiality in accordance with article 32 of the GDPR.

All data and preference analysis activities will not be carried out in an exclusively automated manner, but will always involve the intervention and assessment of duly authorised natural persons.

Your personal data may also be processed by third parties in the following categories:

  • Companies in charge of managing the company's information systems
  • Companies entrusted with the management of services functional to the payment of purchases
  • Legal, tax and accounting consultants
  • Companies in charge of shipping and delivering products
  • Companies providing services instrumental to the management of the contractual relationship (e.g. e-commerce platform providers)

You may request a list of the data processors at any time by writing to unsubscribe@stiga.com. Your personal data will not be disclosed.

7 Your Rights as a Data Subject

As provided for under articles 15 to 21 of the GDPR, you may exercise the following rights by contacting the Company at unsubscribe@stiga.com:

  • Right of access (art. 15): obtain confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning you are being processed, and access to that data and related information.
  • Right to rectification (art. 16): obtain the rectification of inaccurate or incomplete personal data without undue delay.
  • Right to erasure / right to be forgotten (art. 17): obtain the erasure of personal data when they are no longer necessary, consent is withdrawn, processing is unlawful, or other specific conditions are met.
  • Right to restriction of processing (art. 18): obtain the restriction of processing when accuracy is contested, processing is unlawful, or you have objected to processing.
  • Right to data portability (art. 20): receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format, and transmit those data to another controller.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: submit a complaint to the Italian Data Protection Authority at Piazza di Monte Citorio No. 121 – 00186 Rome; e-mail: protocollo@pec.gpdp.it, or to the supervisory authority of your place of regular residence.
